## 2. Core product narrative

LyraAlpha is a financial intelligence platform that helps users understand markets more clearly, act more consistently, and think more structurally.

The central product idea is:

> **The engines compute. The AI interprets.**

That means LyraAlpha is not a generic chatbot wrapped around market data. It is a structured intelligence system that combines deterministic signals with AI interpretation.

### What LyraAlpha does

- Computes market intelligence from structured signals
- Explains what the signals mean in plain language
- Helps users compare, inspect, and contextualize assets
- Supports portfolio intelligence and premium analytical workflows
- Separates market-analysis behavior from support behavior

### What LyraAlpha is not

- Not a brokerage
- Not a personalized financial advisor
- Not a free-form market hallucination engine
- Not a single undifferentiated AI assistant

## 3. Product pillars

### 3.1 Structured intelligence

LyraAlpha’s foundation is deterministic market computation.

The product works with structured signals such as:

- Trend
- Momentum
- Volatility
- Liquidity
- Trust
- Sentiment
- regime context
- compatibility-style overlays and derived intelligence

Marketing implication:

- Lead with clarity, not hype
- Explain that the AI sits on top of computed signals
- Position the product as disciplined and trustworthy

### 3.2 AI interpretation

The AI layer explains the computed context in a way users can act on.

This matters because most market products either:

- overwhelm users with raw data, or
- let AI sound smart without grounding it in stable analysis

LyraAlpha’s value is that it interprets after computation.

### 3.3 Dual-agent separation

LyraAlpha uses two distinct AI roles:

- **Lyra** for market intelligence
- **Myra** for support and navigation

This separation is intentional and should be preserved in all marketing.

### 3.4 Premium workflows

LyraAlpha is not only about shorter answers.

The premium value comes from workflows like:

- Compare Assets
- Shock Simulator
- portfolio intelligence
- briefing surfaces
- score interpretation and regime-aware framing

Marketing implication:

- Sell workflows, not just features
- Show outcomes, not model jargon

## 4. Agent roles

### 4.1 Lyra

Lyra is the market-intelligence agent.

She handles:

- asset analysis
- score interpretation
- regime-aware framing
- premium analytical workflows
- deeper market synthesis for higher-value questions

Lyra should always be described as an interpreter of structured context, not a creator of fictional market facts.

### 4.2 Myra

Myra is the platform-support agent.

She handles:

- plans
- credits
- billing and help flows
- navigation and product guidance
- onboarding and support-style clarification

Myra should stay short, direct, and helpful.

She should redirect market-analysis questions to Lyra.

### 4.3 Messaging rule for both agents

- Lyra = analysis
- Myra = support

Do not merge them in the brand story.

## 17. Product facts marketers should remember

Keep these facts current in every launch, sales, and product-marketing asset:

- LyraAlpha is a multi-asset intelligence platform, not a generic AI wrapper.
- The two AI surfaces are intentionally separated: Lyra for analysis, Myra for support.
- The product is region-aware across US and India.
- Premium workflows matter as much as the core dashboard.
- Market interpretation should always be framed as grounded in computed signals.
- The product supports both public prelaunch guidance and authenticated dashboard experiences.

### Runtime facts worth reflecting in marketing

- The platform runs on GPT-5.4 across nano, mini, and full roles.
- The runtime is plan-aware and query-complexity-aware.
- All Lyra generation paths are fully streaming.
- Reasoning effort is intentionally kept at `none`.
- Output quality is enforced through prompt contracts and verification behavior rather than model-family switching.

These details matter because they shape what the product can honestly claim about speed, depth, and reliability.

### Common objections and responses

#### “Is this just another AI chatbot?”

Response: No. LyraAlpha computes structured signals first and uses AI to interpret them.

#### “How is this different from a brokerage?”

Response: It is not a brokerage. It is a market intelligence platform.

#### “Why should I trust it?”

Response: The product separates computation, interpretation, and support into a structured system.

#### “Why does it have two agents?”

#### “What do I get beyond chat?”

Response: Dashboard intelligence, portfolio intelligence, asset comparison, and premium workflows like Shock Simulator.

Always answer objections with product behavior, not hype.
