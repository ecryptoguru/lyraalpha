# Blog Content Calendar

## Content goal

Create a blog system that drives SEO, GEO, trust, and conversion.

The blog should do four jobs at the same time:

- attract qualified organic traffic
- become citeable for AI search and generative answers
- teach users how to think about the product category
- move readers toward signup, briefing access, or trial activation

## Publishing rhythm

- 2 pillar posts per week
- 1 comparison or use-case post per week
- 1 original insight or data post per week
- 1 product education or conversion post per week
- 1 refresh / update of an older post every 2 weeks

## Editorial standards

Every post should include:

- one clear question or promise in the title
- a direct answer in the first 2-3 paragraphs
- a clean H1/H2 structure
- at least one table, checklist, framework, or visual concept
- one concrete takeaway readers can act on
- one CTA tied to the post intent
- internal links to relevant pillar pages and product pages

## Topic clusters

- Market regime and macro interpretation
- Portfolio risk and drawdown
- Watchlists and alerts
- AI for investing and research
- Fintech product education
- Comparison and alternatives pages
- Daily briefing and signal interpretation
- GEO / AI-search visibility for fintech brands

## Keyword mapping workflow

Use the regional keyword docs as the source of truth for topic selection and page type decisions:

- `50-SEO-IN.md` for India retail investor intent
- `50-SEO-US.md` for US retail investor intent
- `100-SEO-pages.md` for the page-level execution roadmap

Every article, tool page, or comparison page should be mapped to one of these outcomes:

- answer a question directly
- capture a high-intent keyword cluster
- support a tool page with context or instructions
- support a comparison page with education and proof
- support a regional search variant for India or US

## Page-type decision model

- **Blog post** when the query needs explanation, context, or framework
- **Tool page** when the query is action-oriented and can be solved interactively
- **Comparison page** when the user is deciding between products or approaches
- **Answer page** when the search query is direct, specific, and question-based
- **Regional variant** when the query differs materially between India and US intent

## Recommended article structure

### 1. Hook

Start with a direct statement or question.

### 2. Problem framing

Explain why the issue matters in practical terms.

### 3. Core answer

Give the main idea early and clearly.

### 4. Supporting framework

Use examples, tables, lists, or diagrams.

### 5. Product connection

Explain how InsightAlpha helps solve or simplify the problem.

### 6. CTA

Ask for one specific action:

- subscribe to the briefing
- create a watchlist
- try portfolio intelligence
- join the waitlist
- download the research guide

## SEO/GEO content rules

- Use clear H1 and H2 structure
- Put the answer in the first 2-3 paragraphs
- Add definition boxes and FAQs
- Include statistics, tables, and examples
- Use consistent entity language: market intelligence, regime analysis, portfolio risk, daily briefing
- Add internal links between pillar pages and product pages
- Write sections so they can be quoted by AI systems without losing meaning
- Avoid vague filler like “in today’s fast-paced world”

## Conversion focus by content type

- **Pillar posts:** traffic + authority
- **Comparison posts:** high-intent conversion
- **Use-case posts:** activation
- **Original research:** backlinks + PR + AI citations
- **Product education:** signup and retention

## SEO content planning rule

For every week, choose at least one article or page from each of these buckets:

- one India-focused keyword target
- one US-focused keyword target
- one shared keyword target
- one tool or answer page based on search intent

## Content repurposing rules

Every strong blog post should become:

- 1 X thread
- 2-3 X short posts
- 1 LinkedIn post
- 1 LinkedIn carousel or document post
- 1 email recap
- 1 Reddit discussion angle if relevant
- 1 internal FAQ or product help snippet if useful

## Blog performance review

Every week, track:

- traffic by post
- average time on page
- CTA click rate
- email opt-ins
- assisted signups
- backlinks and mentions
- AI visibility / citation opportunities

Then decide:

- which topics to expand
- which posts to refresh
- which titles to improve
- which CTA converts best
