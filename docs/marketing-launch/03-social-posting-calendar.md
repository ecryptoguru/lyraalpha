# Twitter/X + LinkedIn Posting Calendar

## Objective

Build audience, credibility, and product demand through founder-led distribution.

The goal is not just reach. The goal is:

- familiarity
- trust
- repeated exposure
- direct site visits
- signups and activations
- partner attention

## Core distribution rule

Each piece of content should be repurposed into multiple social assets.

A single strong idea should become:

- 1 X thread
- 2-3 X short posts
- 1 LinkedIn post
- 1 LinkedIn carousel or document
- 1 community comment or discussion angle
- 1 email snippet if it performs well

## Weekly cadence

### X / Twitter

- 2-3 posts per day
- 3-4 threads per week
- 15-20 meaningful comments per day on relevant accounts
- 1 chart or screenshot post every 1-2 days
- 1 founder insight post every day

### LinkedIn

- 4 brand posts per week
- 3 founder posts per week
- 1 carousel or document post per week
- daily comments on fintech, investing, and SaaS posts
- 1 product proof or lesson post every week

## Content strategy by platform

### X / Twitter

Best for:

- fast market commentary
- contrarian takes
- chart-based observations
- build-in-public updates
- founder voice
- threads that compress complexity into useful insight

### LinkedIn

Best for:

- educational frameworks

- partner and investor visibility

## 90-day social calendar

### Weeks 1-2

- Post founder intro and product positioning
- Share 2 short market insights daily on X
- Publish 2 LinkedIn posts on why LyraAlpha exists
- Comment daily in fintech and investing communities
- Share 1 build-in-public update per week
- align the first social posts to the highest-priority India and US keyword clusters so the market message and SEO roadmap stay consistent
- Test 3-5 different post hooks across both platforms
- Identify which tone performs best: analytical, explanatory, contrarian, or product-led

### Weeks 3-4

- Launch first educational threads on X
- Publish one LinkedIn carousel per week
- Share screenshots of the product in action
- Post one customer/problem explainer per week
- Start weekly “What changed in markets today?” posts
- turn the strongest weekly keyword themes into social hooks, then into blog and tool-page requests
- Use one recurring visual format for the brand
- Build a consistent commentary rhythm

### Weeks 5-8

- Increase thread frequency around market themes
- Publish founder commentary on launch learnings
- Repurpose blog posts into short posts and carousels
- Share product clips, charts, and briefing snippets
- Highlight user quotes, feedback, and milestones
- Ask direct questions to drive comments and replies
- Start saving high-performing post structures for reuse

### Weeks 9-12

- Run social proof campaigns
- Post mini case studies
- Push referral and newsletter CTAs more directly
- Publish weekly launch recap threads
- Share flagship report snippets across both channels
- Use urgency and momentum without sounding spammy
- Make the product feel like a category movement, not a one-off app

## CTA rules

- X: CTA only after value is delivered
- LinkedIn: CTA should be softer and trust-driven
- Never sound salesy on Reddit-style insights
- Use one clear action per post
- Match CTA to intent: briefing, signup, lead magnet, demo, or reply

## Hook formulas

### X hooks

- “Most people misunderstand ___.”
- “If you’re tracking ___, you’re probably missing ___.”
- “Here’s what changed today that actually matters.”
- “The real problem isn’t ___, it’s ___.”
- “This is the simplest way to think about ___.”

### LinkedIn hooks

- “We kept seeing the same problem, so we built ___.”
- “A better way to think about ___.”
- “What we learned while building LyraAlpha.”
- “Why this matters for investors and fintech teams.”
- “The most useful framework I’ve seen for ___.”

## Repurposing workflow

Each blog post should become:

- 1 X thread
- 2-3 X short posts
- 1 LinkedIn post
- 1 LinkedIn carousel or document post
- 1 email recap
- 1 Reddit discussion angle if relevant
- 1 founder comment responding to audience questions

## Engagement strategy

### Daily comment targets

- 10-15 thoughtful comments on X
- 5-10 thoughtful comments on LinkedIn
- 1-3 useful comments on Reddit or communities

### Comment style

- add context
- answer questions
- use examples
- avoid generic praise
- avoid dropping links unless directly relevant

## Measurement

Weekly social review should track:

- impressions
- engagement rate
- follower growth
- profile visits
- site clicks
- assisted signups
- comments and saves
- best-performing hooks
- best-performing CTA types

## Optimization rule

Double down on the post format that drives the most qualified site visits or signups, not just the most likes.
