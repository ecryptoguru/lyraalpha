# KPI Dashboard and Growth Funnel Model

## Growth objective

Track the path to 10,000 crypto-native users in 90 days with a clear funnel and weekly operating metrics.

This dashboard should answer four questions every week:

1. Where is awareness coming from?
2. Which content and channels create engagement?
3. Which paths actually convert (wallet connections)?
4. What improves activation and retention (7-9 touchpoint journey)?

## Funnel model

### Stage 1: Awareness

This is the top of the funnel. The goal is to be seen by the right audience repeatedly.

#### Sources

- SEO and GEO (AI answerability)
- X / Twitter (crypto-native tone)
- LinkedIn (platform-native, links in comments)
- Reddit (90/10 value-to-promotion discipline)
- Discord / Telegram (24/7 moderation, scam prevention)
- PR and newsletter mentions
- Crypto partnerships
- Founder-led shares
- AI citations / mentions (GEO performance)

#### Key metrics

- impressions (vanity metric, secondary)
- organic sessions
- social reach
- referral traffic
- AI mention / citation rate (GEO performance)
- AI overview appearances
- AI search visibility metrics
- Backlinks (quality over quantity)
- SEO impressions by India keyword cluster (crypto-specific)
- SEO impressions by US keyword cluster (crypto-specific)
- Branded search volume (trust signal)
- Follower growth (crypto community quality)
- On-chain wallet connection rate

#### Interpretation rules

- high impressions with low clicks usually means the hook or title is weak (or not GEO-optimized)
- high traffic from one channel with poor conversion means the audience is misaligned or the page is weak (7-9 touchpoint journey context)
- strong branded search suggests trust and recall are increasing (addressing 96% token failures trust deficit)
- AI citation rate indicates GEO optimization success
- Wallet connection rate is more valuable than page views for crypto

### Stage 2: Engagement

This stage measures whether people are paying attention and consuming value.

#### Sources

- blog posts (GEO-optimized with direct answers)
- social threads (platform-native formats)
- webinars (addressing trust concerns)
- crypto market reports (original data)
- lead magnets (crypto intelligence, not generic fintech)
- product explainers (demonstrated utility)
- briefings (regime awareness)

#### Key metrics

- Time on page
- Scroll depth
- Email opt-in rate
- Content downloads
- Webinar attendance
- Return visitors (trust signal)
- Blog-to-tool page click-through rate
- Answer-page scroll depth (AI answerability)
- Click-through from social (platform-native)
- Comments and shares (AI citation potential)
- Reddit 90/10 compliance (discipline adherence)
- Discord/Telegram community health (moderation quality)

#### Interpretation rules

- high traffic but low scroll depth usually means the intro or structure is weak (or not GEO-optimized)
- good engagement but low signups means the CTA or offer needs improvement (trust signals)
- high return visits are a strong early signal of trust (addressing 7-9 touchpoint journey)
- Low AI citation rate indicates need for better GEO optimization

### Stage 3: Conversion

This stage measures whether attention becomes a user, lead, or subscriber.

#### Sources

- Signup flow (wallet connection)
- Newsletter opt-in
- Free trial
- Waitlist
- Demo request
- Lead magnet capture (crypto intelligence)
- Referral invite flow (asymmetric incentives)

#### Key metrics

- Signup conversion rate (wallet connections)
- Signup entry conversion rate (E-E-A-T signals)
- Cost per signup (crypto-native ad networks where permissible)
- Cost per lead
- Trial start rate
- Lead magnet conversion rate
- Tool-page conversion rate (GEO-optimized)
- Comparison-page conversion rate (crypto-specific)
- Demo request rate
- Referral conversion rate
- On-chain wallet connection rate

#### Interpretation rules

- if top-of-funnel is healthy but conversion is weak, fix signup entry pages and CTA clarity (add E-E-A-T signals)
- if conversion is strong but activation is weak, fix onboarding (address 7-9 touchpoint journey)
- if signup conversion is above target, test scaling the channel further (crypto-specific metrics)
- track AI citation rate for GEO performance

### Stage 4: Activation

This stage measures whether the user experiences the product value quickly.

#### Sources

- Onboarding emails (trust-building content)
- In-app guidance (demonstrated utility)
- First briefing read (regime awareness)
- Watchlist creation (on-chain behavior tracking)
- Portfolio connection (wallet connection)
- First saved insight
- First click on a signal or alert

#### Key metrics

- Activation rate
- Time to first value (7-9 touchpoint journey context)
- Onboarding completion
- First-session retention
- Free-to-active rate
- First briefing open rate
- First watchlist creation rate
- First portfolio connection rate (wallet connection)
- First tool interaction rate from SEO signup entry pages
- On-chain behavior tracking (wallet activity)

#### Interpretation rules

- if signups are high but activation is low, the onboarding flow is too abstract (add crypto-specific context)
- if users reach the dashboard but never take action, the product's first-use value is unclear (demonstrated utility)
- a strong activation rate is one of the clearest signs of product-market message fit
- track on-chain behavior for deeper insights

### Stage 5: Retention

This stage measures whether users come back.

#### Sources

- Email sequences (trust-building)
- Daily briefing (regime awareness)
- Alerts (on-chain behavior)
- Repeat visits (trust signal)
- Saved content
- Ongoing crypto market commentary
- Referral reminders

#### Key metrics

- D1 retention
- D7 retention
- D30 retention
- Weekly active users
- Email open rate
- Email click-through rate
- Repeat briefing reads
- Alert click rate (on-chain behavior)
- Repeat visits from SEO signup entry pages (GEO-optimized)
- Returning user rate
- Wallet holder retention rate (on-chain)
- Governance participation percentage

#### Interpretation rules

- retention without activation usually means users are curious but not seeing value (7-9 touchpoint journey)
- strong D7 but weak D30 suggests the product is useful but not habitual yet (need demonstrated utility)
- email engagement can be a strong proxy for early product relevance
- on-chain retention metrics provide deeper insights than standard web analytics

### Stage 6: Referral

This stage measures whether users bring in other users.

#### Sources

- Invite flow (asymmetric incentives)
- Shareable insights (demonstrated utility)
- Partner campaigns (crypto-native)
- Community mentions (Discord/Telegram/Reddit)
- Social reposts (platform-native)
- Newsletter references

#### Key metrics

- Referral rate
- Invites sent per user
- Invite conversion rate
- Viral coefficient
- Share rate
- Reshare rate
- Partner-sourced signups (crypto-native)
- Backlinks from SEO-driven content (quality over quantity)
- On-chain wallet attribution where applicable

#### Interpretation rules

- low referral rate means users are not yet proud or motivated enough to share (need demonstrated utility)
- high share rate with low click-through may mean the content is interesting but not actionable (add clear CTAs)
- referral traffic often indicates stronger trust than pure paid traffic (addressing 96% token failures trust deficit)
- on-chain attribution provides verifiable referral tracking

## Target benchmarks for 90 days

These are working targets, not promises. Adjust by region and channel quality.

- Organic traffic growth: 20-30% month-over-month after week 4
- Signup conversion rate: 3-8% depending on signup entry page (wallet connections)
- Email opt-in rate: 20-40% on lead magnets (crypto intelligence)
- Activation rate: 30-50% of signups (7-9 touchpoint journey)
- Referral contribution: 5-15% of total signups by day 90 (asymmetric incentives)
- Returning visitor rate: steadily rising after week 3 (trust signal)
- AI citation rate: steadily increasing after week 4 (GEO performance)
- One or more content assets should drive at least 15-20% of weekly signups once the funnel stabilizes
- On-chain wallet connection rate: tracking alongside web metrics

## Funnel health rules

- If traffic is high but signups are low, fix signup entry pages (add E-E-A-T signals, trust markers)
- If signups are high but activation is low, fix onboarding (address 7-9 touchpoint journey)
- If activation is strong but retention is weak, improve ongoing value (demonstrated utility)
- If organic is weak, increase content quality and distribution (GEO optimization)
- If social is weak, improve hooks and repurposing (platform-native adaptation)
- If referrals are weak, make sharing easier and more rewarding (asymmetric incentives)
- If one channel dominates but quality is weak, rebalance toward better-fit channels (crypto-specific metrics)
- If AI citation rate is low, improve GEO optimization (direct answers, semantic depth)
- If community health is weak, improve moderation and scam prevention (24/7 coverage)

## Decision rules

### When to scale a channel

Scale a channel if it has:

- consistent traffic
- acceptable CAC (crypto-native ad networks where permissible)
- above-target activation (wallet connections)
- strong engagement quality (trust signals)
- repeatable output (platform-native)
- strong AI citation rate (GEO performance)
- positive on-chain behavior tracking

### When to stop or pause a channel

Pause or reduce a channel if it has:

- high cost and low activation (wallet connections)
- lots of clicks but poor signups (misaligned audience)
- poor audience fit (not crypto-native)
- weak trust signals (addressing 96% token failures)
- no repeatability
- low AI citation rate (GEO not optimized)
- community health issues (moderation, scam prevention)
