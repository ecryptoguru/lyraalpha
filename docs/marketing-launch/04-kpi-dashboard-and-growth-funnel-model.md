# KPI Dashboard and Growth Funnel Model

## Growth objective

Track the path to 10,000 users in 90 days with a clear funnel and weekly operating metrics.

This dashboard should answer four questions every week:

1. Where is awareness coming from?
2. Which content and channels create engagement?
3. Which paths actually convert?
4. What improves activation and retention?

## Funnel model

### Stage 1: Awareness

This is the top of the funnel. The goal is to be seen by the right audience repeatedly.

#### Sources

- SEO and GEO
- X / Twitter
- LinkedIn
- Reddit
- PR and newsletter mentions
- Partnerships
- founder-led shares
- AI citations / mentions

#### Key metrics

- impressions
- organic sessions
- social reach
- referral traffic
- AI mention / citation rate
- backlinks
- SEO impressions by India keyword cluster
- SEO impressions by US keyword cluster
- branded search volume
- follower growth

#### Interpretation rules

- high impressions with low clicks usually means the hook or title is weak
- high traffic from one channel with poor conversion means the audience is misaligned or the page is weak
- strong branded search suggests trust and recall are increasing

### Stage 2: Engagement

This stage measures whether people are paying attention and consuming value.

#### Sources

- blog posts
- social threads
- webinars
- market reports
- lead magnets
- product explainers
- briefings

#### Key metrics

- time on page
- scroll depth
- email opt-in rate
- content downloads
- webinar attendance
- return visitors
- blog-to-tool page click-through rate
- answer-page scroll depth
- click-through from social
- comments and shares

#### Interpretation rules

- high traffic but low scroll depth usually means the intro or structure is weak
- good engagement but low signups means the CTA or offer needs improvement
- high return visits are a strong early signal of trust

### Stage 3: Conversion

This stage measures whether attention becomes a user, lead, or subscriber.

#### Sources

- signup flow
- newsletter opt-in
- free trial
- waitlist
- demo request
- lead magnet capture
- referral invite flow

#### Key metrics

- signup conversion rate
- landing page conversion rate
- cost per signup
- cost per lead
- trial start rate
- lead magnet conversion rate
- tool-page conversion rate
- comparison-page conversion rate
- demo request rate
- referral conversion rate

#### Interpretation rules

- if top-of-funnel is healthy but conversion is weak, fix landing pages and CTA clarity
- if conversion is strong but activation is weak, fix onboarding
- if signup conversion is above target, test scaling the channel further

### Stage 4: Activation

This stage measures whether the user experiences the product value quickly.

#### Sources

- onboarding emails
- in-app guidance
- first briefing read
- watchlist creation
- portfolio connection
- first saved insight
- first click on a signal or alert

#### Key metrics

- activation rate
- time to first value
- onboarding completion
- first-session retention
- free-to-active rate
- first briefing open rate
- first watchlist creation rate
- first portfolio connection rate
- first tool interaction rate from SEO landing pages

#### Interpretation rules

- if signups are high but activation is low, the onboarding flow is too abstract
- if users reach the dashboard but never take action, the product’s first-use value is unclear
- a strong activation rate is one of the clearest signs of product-market message fit

### Stage 5: Retention

This stage measures whether users come back.

#### Sources

- email sequences
- daily briefing
- alerts
- repeat visits
- saved content
- ongoing market commentary
- referral reminders

#### Key metrics

- D1 retention
- D7 retention
- D30 retention
- weekly active users
- email open rate
- email click-through rate
- repeat briefing reads
- alert click rate
- repeat visits from SEO landing pages
- returning user rate

#### Interpretation rules

- retention without activation usually means users are curious but not seeing value
- strong D7 but weak D30 suggests the product is useful but not habitual yet
- email engagement can be a strong proxy for early product relevance

### Stage 6: Referral

This stage measures whether users bring in other users.

#### Sources

- invite flow
- shareable insights
- partner campaigns
- community mentions
- social reposts
- newsletter references

#### Key metrics

- referral rate
- invites sent per user
- invite conversion rate
- viral coefficient
- share rate
- reshare rate
- partner-sourced signups
- backlinks from SEO-driven content

#### Interpretation rules

- low referral rate means users are not yet proud or motivated enough to share
- high share rate with low click-through may mean the content is interesting but not actionable
- referral traffic often indicates stronger trust than pure paid traffic

## Target benchmarks for 90 days

These are working targets, not promises. Adjust by region and channel quality.

- Organic traffic growth: 20-30% month-over-month after week 4
- Signup conversion rate: 3-8% depending on landing page
- Email opt-in rate: 20-40% on lead magnets
- Activation rate: 30-50% of signups
- Referral contribution: 5-15% of total signups by day 90
- Returning visitor rate: steadily rising after week 3
- One or more content assets should drive at least 15-20% of weekly signups once the funnel stabilizes

## Funnel health rules

- If traffic is high but signups are low, fix landing pages
- If signups are high but activation is low, fix onboarding
- If activation is strong but retention is weak, improve ongoing value
- If organic is weak, increase content quality and distribution
- If social is weak, improve hooks and repurposing
- If referrals are weak, make sharing easier and more rewarding
- If one channel dominates but quality is weak, rebalance toward better-fit channels

## Decision rules

### When to scale a channel

Scale a channel if it has:

- consistent traffic
- acceptable CAC
- above-target activation
- strong engagement quality
- repeatable output

### When to stop or pause a channel

Pause or reduce a channel if it has:

- high cost and low activation
- lots of clicks but poor signups
- poor audience fit
- weak trust signals
- no repeatability

## Reporting format

Every weekly report should include:

- what changed this week
- what grew
- what converted best
- what underperformed
- what you are changing next week
- one sentence summary of the current growth thesis
