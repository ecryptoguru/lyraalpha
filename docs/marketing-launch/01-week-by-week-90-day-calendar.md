# Week-by-Week 90-Day Launch Calendar

## Goal

Reach 10,000 users in 90 days through a phased fintech SaaS launch built on SEO/GEO, founder-led social, community distribution, partnerships, referral loops, and product-led conversion.

## What this calendar is designed to do

This plan is not just a publishing schedule. It is the operating system for launch.

Each week should:

- increase brand trust
- create one or more new acquisition surfaces
- improve conversion on the existing funnel
- create reusable content assets
- surface proof, authority, or urgency
- push users one step deeper into activation

## Strategy pillars

- **Trust-led positioning** — market intelligence, risk, and clarity
- **Content engine** — SEO + GEO + educational blog system
- **Founder-led distribution** — X and LinkedIn as primary attention channels
- **Community distribution** — Reddit and niche communities for authenticity
- **Conversion system** — landing pages, lead magnets, email onboarding, and referral loops
- **Partnership leverage** — newsletters, creators, fintech communities, and ecosystem partners
- **Product-led loops** — shareable insights, referrals, onboarding, and ongoing email value
- **SEO keyword routing** — use `50-SEO-IN.md` and `50-SEO-US.md` to map search intent into `100-SEO-pages.md`, blog posts, and tool pages

## Launch phase model

### Phase 1: Foundation and positioning (Weeks 1-2)

The first two weeks should create the minimum viable growth stack:

- a clear ICP
- a strong promise
- a homepage with conversion-ready messaging
- a blog engine with search and AI visibility structure
- a basic social distribution engine
- a measurable funnel
- a lead magnet and email sequence

### Phase 2: Content and distribution launch (Weeks 3-4)

The next two weeks should start pushing signals into the market:

- publish SEO/GEO content consistently
- turn content into social assets
- begin outreach to creators and newsletters
- collect early feedback and testimonials
- identify which messages earn attention and conversions
- build the first keyword-targeted pages from the India/US keyword maps
- decide which queries deserve blog posts, which deserve tool pages, and which deserve comparison pages

### Phase 3: Scale the winners (Weeks 5-8)

This phase is about compounding:

- double down on the best topics
- build comparison and use-case pages
- run live sessions and webinars
- launch referrals
- start retargeting
- improve onboarding based on behavior

### Phase 4: Conversion and amplification (Weeks 9-12)

The final month should maximize:

- high-intent traffic
- social proof
- referral momentum
- community trust
- conversion rate improvements
- partner amplification

## Week 1 — Positioning, ICP, and tracking setup

### Objectives

- define the market category and product promise
- ensure analytics are ready before launch traffic starts
- set the message architecture for site, blog, and social

### Key deliverables

- finalize the primary ICP and secondary segments
- decide whether the launch CTA is signup, briefing, waitlist, or demo
- write the one-line positioning statement
- define 3 product value pillars
- map the first 10 content topics
- set up analytics, attribution, and conversion events
- create the first version of the signup and lead capture pages
- build social bios and consistent profile messaging

### Detailed execution checklist

- homepage hero should answer: what is LyraAlpha, who is it for, and why now
- the CTA should be visible above the fold and repeated near proof sections
- add a simple trust layer: founder bio, product claims, and a short explanation of the engine/AI split
- create a reusable content brief template for all future blog posts
- define a single weekly KPI dashboard before publishing starts

### Success criteria

- attribution is working
- homepage messaging is aligned
- signup flow is live
- social profiles are consistent
- content backlog is ready

---

## Week 2 — Pillar content and funnel skeleton

### Objectives

- publish foundational content
- create extractable SEO/GEO structure
- prepare onboarding and lead nurturing

### Key deliverables

- publish the first 3 pillar blog posts
- create FAQ and glossary blocks
- add schema-ready content structure
- build a welcome email sequence
- draft a founder story / launch narrative
- start daily social posting
- begin light Reddit participation with value-first comments and posts
- create the first SEO page backlog using `50-SEO-IN.md`, `50-SEO-US.md`, and `100-SEO-pages.md`
- **SEO keyword mapping**: map 10 high-priority keywords from the India/US keyword docs to specific blog posts and tool pages

### Detailed execution checklist

- every pillar page should have:
  - definition
  - why it matters
  - how it works
  - examples
  - related links
  - CTA
- build one lead magnet aligned to the core promise, not a generic fintech PDF
- create onboarding emails that drive the first aha moment
- make sure the signup path has low friction and no unnecessary fields
- start collecting qualitative user questions from social and comments

### Success criteria

- first content pieces are live
- the funnel can capture interest
- the product story is understandable in under 10 seconds
- early audience signals are coming in

## Week 3 — First distribution wave

### Objectives

- begin building real reach
- turn content into distribution assets
- test message resonance across channels

### Key deliverables

- publish 2 educational blog posts and 1 comparison post
- ship the first original data or insight post
- launch weekly founder commentary on X
- publish the first LinkedIn carousel
- begin outreach to newsletters and creators
- test 2-3 signup CTA variants
- publish the first 2-3 tool or answer pages that directly target high-value keywords from the regional keyword maps
- **SEO page roadmap**: identify 5 key pages from the 100-page SEO roadmap and prioritize them for the next 2 weeks

### Detailed execution checklist

- take every blog post and turn it into:
  - 1 X thread
  - 2 short X posts
  - 1 LinkedIn post
  - 1 LinkedIn comment angle
  - 1 Reddit discussion angle if relevant
- identify which headlines drive clicks vs which drive saves/shares
- capture social responses in a feedback doc
- start tracking which distribution channels create activated users, not just visitors

### Success criteria

- first organic traction appears
- a few people engage repeatedly
- one or two external accounts start noticing the brand
- the team learns which angle resonates most

## Week 5 — Scale the best themes

### Objectives

- double down on what worked
- start building repeatable traffic patterns

### Key deliverables

- publish comparison pages and use-case pages
- publish one market report or research post
- improve onboarding emails based on behavior
- start retargeting ads for engaged visitors
- expand social coverage around the best-performing ideas
- expand the SEO cluster around the best-performing keywords from India and US search intent
- add internal links from blogs into the corresponding tool or answer pages

### Detailed execution checklist

- find the content type with the highest signup rate
- find the social format with the highest engagement rate
- find the CTA that converts best
- build a small retargeting audience from readers and visitors
- optimize the signup page to reduce hesitation

### Success criteria

- a winning content format emerges
- traffic starts compounding from multiple angles
- retargeting can now reinforce the funnel

## Week 6 — Community expansion and referral hooks

### Objectives

- grow community presence
- build first referral loop
- increase product usefulness after signup

### Key deliverables

- publish 2 blog posts and 1 newsletter-style insight post
- ship one product education page
- run a second AMA or live briefing
- expand Reddit participation into 2-3 relevant communities
- add a referral incentive to the product

### Detailed execution checklist

- referral incentive should be tied to value, not just vanity rewards
- community participation should be useful, not promotional
- build one easy sharing moment inside the product
- add a simple invite prompt inside onboarding or briefing flows

### Success criteria

- early users invite others
- community presence starts to look natural
- the product has at least one built-in growth loop

## Week 7 — Proof and credibility

### Objectives

- increase authority
- show proof of value
- improve conversion rate with trust assets

### Key deliverables

- publish one flagship SEO article
- publish one founder narrative post on LinkedIn
- launch social proof assets: quotes, screenshots, mini case studies
- secure 2-3 partner mentions or cross-promotions
- optimize landing pages for conversion rate

### Detailed execution checklist

- add screenshots that show product clarity, not feature clutter
- add quote cards to landing pages and social posts
- turn one strong user quote into a broader proof story
- reach out to partners with a very specific collaboration ask

### Success criteria

- partner replies start to come in
- conversion rate improves on the strongest pages

### Objectives

- widen trust through third-party validation
- expand reach via partner channels

### Key deliverables

- publish one strong comparison article
- publish one user story or mini case study
- launch a partner bundle or co-marketing offer

### Detailed execution checklist

- turn the Q&A into clips and blog snippets

### Key deliverables

- run Reddit recap posts that summarize useful insights

### Objectives

- close the 90-day loop

### Key deliverables

## Weekly operating cadence

- **Monday:** publish or draft one high-intent article; set weekly targets
- **Tuesday:** founder commentary on X and LinkedIn; comment on community posts
- **Wednesday:** community engagement + Reddit value post; review message resonance
- **Thursday:** social repurposing + email send; update landing page copy if needed
- **Friday:** live session, AMA, or insight roundup; collect questions and objections
- **Saturday:** comment engagement and light social touchpoints; schedule next content
- **Sunday:** metrics review and next-week planning; reassign priorities

## Operating rules

- Publish every week without exception
- Repurpose every strong asset across at least 3 channels
- Focus on the channel that shows the best signup quality, not just the highest reach
- Improve one conversion point every week
- Treat social proof as a growth asset, not a nice-to-have
- Make the product easier to explain every week

## Success checkpoints

- **Week 2:** tracking and funnel fully live
- **Week 4:** first content traction and first partner mentions
- **Week 8:** repeatable content + distribution pattern established
- **Week 12:** scalable launch system proven
