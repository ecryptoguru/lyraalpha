## Key Concept

A portfolio is more than a list of assets; it's a single, complex organism. The **Portfolio Health Meter** evaluates this organism in real-time, providing a single score (0-100) that tells you if your portfolio is robust, resilient, and aligned with the current market, or if it's carrying hidden systemic risks.

It is *not* a performance metric (like your P&L). It is a structural risk assessment.

## How It's Calculated

The Health Score blends four critical dimensions:

1. **Asset Quality (40%)**: The weighted average of the Signal Strength scores for every asset you hold. If you own high-conviction, strong-trending assets, this component scores high.
2. **Regime Alignment (30%)**: Are your holdings fighting the tape? If the market is in a Defensive regime, and your portfolio is 90% high-beta crypto and tech, this score plummets.
3. **Diversification (20%)**: Do you own 15 different AI stocks, or a true mix of sectors and asset classes? This penalizes over-concentration.
4. **Fragility Index (10%)**: A stress-test component measuring how vulnerable your portfolio is to a sudden volatility shock or liquidity drying up.

## What It Means

- **Excellent (80-100)**: Your portfolio is full of high-signal assets, perfectly aligned with the current macro regime, well-diversified, and highly resilient to shocks.
- **Good (60-79)**: Solid structure, but perhaps slightly misaligned with the regime or carrying a few weak assets dragging down the average.
- **Fair (40-59)**: Warning signs. You might be too concentrated, holding assets with deteriorating signals, or fighting the broader market trend.
- **Poor (0-39)**: High structural risk. Your portfolio is likely heavily concentrated in weak, low-signal assets during a hostile market regime.

## The Fragility Index

The Fragility Index (0-100) is the inverse of resilience. A high fragility score (e.g., 85) means your portfolio is a "glass cannon" — it might be performing well right now, but a sudden market shock (like a VIX spike or a rapid regime shift to Risk-Off) will likely cause severe drawdowns because your assets lack defensive characteristics and liquidity.

- [ ] Diversification only cares about how many total assets you own. | No — owning 20 different semiconductor stocks is not true diversification; the engine looks for distinct sectors, asset classes, and risk factors.
