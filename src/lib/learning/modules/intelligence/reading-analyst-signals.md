## Key Concept

Analyst ratings (Buy, Hold, Sell) and Price Targets are ubiquitous in financial media. However, treating them as gospel is a surefire way to underperform.

**Reading Analyst Signals** is about understanding the *trend* of analyst sentiment, the *spread* of their targets, and how the market reacts to their upgrades and downgrades. 

## The Consensus Bar

The Analyst Consensus aggregates all current ratings from major Wall Street firms into a single view:
- **Strong Buy**: High conviction from the majority of covering analysts.
- **Buy**: Positive bias, but some hesitation or "Hold" ratings mixed in.
- **Hold**: Neutral sentiment. Often used by analysts who missed a rally but don't want to issue a Sell rating.
- **Sell / Strong Sell**: Rare and significant. Analysts are inherently biased toward positive ratings to maintain corporate access. A consensus "Sell" is a massive red flag.

## Price Targets & The Spread

A single "Average Price Target" is useless without context. You must look at the **spread**:

1. **Mean/Median Target**: The consensus expectation for the stock's price in 12 months.
2. **High Target**: The most bullish scenario envisioned by the street.
3. **Low Target**: The bear case. This is your margin of safety.

If a stock is trading at $100, the Mean Target is $110, the High is $180, and the Low is $40, you have a **massive spread**. The analysts have no idea what this company is worth. High uncertainty equals high risk.

If the spread is tight ($105 to $115), the market has a very clear expectation of the company's future cash flows.

## Upgrades and Downgrades

The *action* of an upgrade or downgrade is often more important than the rating itself.
- **The Upgrade Cycle**: When one major firm upgrades a stock and raises its price target, others often follow suit over the coming weeks to avoid looking foolish. This creates a powerful sentiment tailwind.
- **The "Sell the News" Downgrade**: If a stock has run up 50% and gets a downgrade from "Buy" to "Hold" because of "valuation concerns," the market often uses it as an excuse to take profits aggressively.

## Quick Check

- [x] A tight spread between the High and Low price targets indicates strong consensus and lower uncertainty. | Correct — it means analysts generally agree on the company's valuation.
- [ ] If a stock has a consensus "Strong Buy" rating, you should immediately buy it. | No — analyst ratings are just one input; they are often late to the party and biased upward.
- [x] A consensus "Sell" rating is rare and usually indicates severe, recognized structural problems. | Yes — analysts are reluctant to issue Sell ratings, so when they do, it's significant.
- [ ] The "Average Price Target" is the exact price the stock will hit in 12 months. | No — it's merely the average of various educated guesses.
