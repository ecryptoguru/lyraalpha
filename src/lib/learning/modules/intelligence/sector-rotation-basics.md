## Key Concept

Capital doesn't just leave the market; it moves *around* the market. **Sector Rotation** is the continuous flow of institutional money from one sector of the economy to another, anticipating the next phase of the macroeconomic cycle.

The Discovery Stocks (Sector Themes) dashboard visualizes this flow in real-time.

## The Traditional Rotation Cycle

While never perfect, sectors historically rotate in a predictable sequence based on the business cycle and market regime:

1. **Early Recovery (Risk-On)**: Interest rates fall, economic growth begins to accelerate. 
   - **Leaders**: Technology, Consumer Discretionary, Financials.
2. **Mid-Cycle (Strong Risk-On)**: Growth peaks, inflation starts to rise.
   - **Leaders**: Industrials, Basic Materials, Energy.
3. **Late Cycle (Transitioning / Defensive)**: Inflation is high, central banks hike rates to cool the economy.
   - **Leaders**: Energy, Healthcare, Consumer Staples.
4. **Recession (Risk-Off)**: Growth turns negative, interest rates peak and begin to fall.
   - **Leaders**: Utilities, Consumer Staples, Bonds.

## Reading the Sector Grid

The Discovery Sector Grid scores every sector (0-100) based on its current Regime Alignment and underlying Signal Strength.

- **High-Scoring Sectors (80+)**: Capital is aggressively flowing into this sector. It is perfectly aligned with the current macro regime.
- **Emerging Leaders**: Sectors whose scores are rapidly accelerating from the 40s/50s into the 60s/70s. This is the "smart money" moving early before the rotation is obvious.
- **Deteriorating Sectors**: Sectors whose scores are falling from 80+ back into the 60s. The momentum is fading, and institutions are distributing their shares to retail investors.

## How to Use It

Don't just buy the highest-scoring sector; look for the *shift*.

If the market has been in a Strong Risk-On regime dominated by Tech, and you suddenly see Utilities and Consumer Staples (Defensive sectors) accelerating rapidly in the Sector Grid, you are witnessing a real-time regime transition. The big money is moving to safety.
