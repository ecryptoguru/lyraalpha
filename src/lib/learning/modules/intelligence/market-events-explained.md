## Key Concept

The market events timeline is the platform's way of tying **catalysts to context**. It helps you see how events, news, and market shocks may relate to changes in scores, narratives, and regime behavior.

It is most useful when you want to answer: *what changed, and why now?*

## What It Means

- Events add timing context to score and narrative changes.
- The timeline is useful for checking whether a move was driven by a broad macro event, a company-specific catalyst, or a regime shift.
- Looking at events after a big move can help you avoid inventing explanations from hindsight.
- Events work best when read alongside the asset page, narratives page, or watchlist.

## What It Does NOT Mean

- Every event on the timeline will matter equally.
- An event appearing on the timeline is **not** a trading instruction.
- The event feed is **not** meant to replace your broader analysis workflow.
