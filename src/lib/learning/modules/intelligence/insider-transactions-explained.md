## Key Concept

Corporate insiders (CEOs, CFOs, Directors) know their businesses better than anyone else. When they buy or sell their own company's stock on the open market, it sends a signal.

However, the old adage applies: **"Insiders sell for many reasons (taxes, diversification, buying a house), but they only buy for one reason: they think the price is going up."**

## What to Look For

InsightAlpha AI tracks insider activity and calculates the **Management Sentiment Percentile Rank (MSPR)**. This score (0-100) measures how bullish or bearish the executive team's recent trading activity is compared to historical norms and sector peers.

1. **Open Market Buys**: The strongest signal. A CEO buying $1M of stock on the open market (not just receiving stock options) is putting their own cash on the line. 
2. **Cluster Buying**: When multiple executives (e.g., the CEO, CFO, and two Directors) all buy stock in the same week. This is an extremely high-conviction signal that the internal data looks fantastic.
3. **Significant Selling**: Routine selling is normal. But if the entire C-suite suddenly dumps 30% of their holdings right before an earnings report or a major product launch, it's a massive red flag.

## The MSPR Score

- **High MSPR (80+)**: Strong insider buying relative to history. Management is highly confident in the company's valuation and trajectory.
- **Neutral MSPR (40-79)**: Normal activity. Routine option exercises and scheduled selling programs (10b5-1 plans).
- **Low MSPR (0-39)**: Heavy insider selling. Management is taking chips off the table aggressively.

## What It Means

Insider buying is a **fundamental tailwind**, not a timing tool. An insider might buy today because they see value over the next 3 years, while the stock continues to drop for the next 3 months. 

Combine high MSPR (insider conviction) with a high Trend Score (market conviction) for the most powerful setups.
