## Key Concept

A **market regime** describes the dominant behavioral state of the market at any given time. Our Multi-Horizon Regime Detection Engine (MRDE) classifies the market into four primary states:

- **Risk-On** — Investors are confident, buying equities and growth assets. Volatility is low, breadth is wide, and momentum is positive.
- **Neutral** — No strong directional bias. The market is transitioning or consolidating. Mixed signals across indicators.
- **Defensive** — Early signs of caution. Investors rotate toward safer assets (bonds, utilities, gold). Breadth narrows but no panic.
- **Risk-Off** — Fear dominates. Broad selling, high volatility, flight to safety. Correlations spike as "everything sells together."

The regime isn't a prediction — it's a classification of current conditions based on VIX levels, market breadth, sector rotation patterns, and cross-asset correlations.

## What It Means

Different assets behave very differently depending on the regime:

- **Growth stocks** tend to outperform in Risk-On and underperform in Risk-Off.
- **Gold and bonds** tend to outperform in Defensive and Risk-Off environments.
- **Commodities** are mixed — energy does well in Risk-On, while precious metals shine in Risk-Off.
- **Crypto** is highly regime-sensitive — it tends to amplify whatever the equity market is doing.

The MRDE also computes a **transition probability** — the likelihood that the current regime will shift to a different state. A high transition probability means the market is at an inflection point, which is when the most interesting opportunities (and risks) emerge.

Each asset gets an **ARCS (Asset Regime Compatibility Score)** that measures how well-positioned it is for the current regime. A defensive stock in a Risk-On regime will have a low ARCS, even if its fundamentals are strong.

## What It Does NOT Mean

- The regime classification is **not** a market forecast. "Risk-On" doesn't mean the market will keep going up — it means conditions currently favor risk-taking.
- Regime transitions are **not** instant. Markets can spend weeks in a transitional state between regimes. Don't expect clean, binary switches.
- A "Risk-Off" regime does **not** mean you should sell everything. It means defensive positioning has historically outperformed in similar conditions.
- ARCS is **not** a quality score. A great company can have a low ARCS simply because the current regime doesn't favor its characteristics.
