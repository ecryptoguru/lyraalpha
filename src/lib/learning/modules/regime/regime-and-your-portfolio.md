## Key Concept

Different asset classes behave predictably differently across market regimes. Understanding these patterns doesn't tell you *when* regimes will change, but it tells you *what to expect* from your portfolio in each environment. This is the bridge between regime awareness and portfolio decision-making.

The four primary regimes — **Risk-On**, **Risk-Off**, **Transitional**, and **Crisis** — each create distinct winners and losers across stocks, bonds, commodities, crypto, and cash.

## What It Means

- **Risk-On Regime**: Equities (especially growth and small-cap) outperform. Crypto rallies. Commodities tied to industrial demand (copper, oil) strengthen. Bonds underperform. Volatility is low. This is the "everything works" environment — but it breeds complacency.
- **Risk-Off Regime**: Defensive assets outperform — government bonds, gold, utility stocks, high-quality dividend payers. Growth stocks, crypto, and industrial commodities weaken. Volatility rises. Cash becomes attractive.
- **Transitional Regime**: The most dangerous period. Correlations break down, historical patterns fail, and assets that "always" move together start diverging. Factor rotations (growth → value, or vice versa) happen here. Diversification is most valuable during transitions.
- **Crisis Regime**: Correlation convergence — nearly everything falls as investors sell for liquidity. Only the highest-quality government bonds and cash hold value. Gold may initially fall (liquidity selling) before rallying. Recovery from crisis regimes often creates the strongest returns for those who stayed invested.

The key portfolio insight: your portfolio's regime sensitivity determines how much your returns depend on *which* regime we're in. A portfolio that only works in risk-on is a bet on regime persistence, not a diversified strategy.

## What It Does NOT Mean

- Knowing the current regime does **not** tell you when it will change. Regimes can persist for months or shift in days.
- Asset class behavior in each regime is **not** guaranteed. These are historical tendencies with meaningful exceptions.
- Regime awareness is **not** a timing tool. It's a framework for understanding *why* your portfolio is behaving the way it is and whether your risk exposure matches your intentions.
