## Key Concept

Assets don't exist in a vacuum; they exist within a **Market Regime**. An aggressive growth stock might look fantastic on a chart, but if the broader market is in a Defensive regime, that stock is swimming against a massive macroeconomic tide.

**Factor Alignment** (powered by the ARCS engine) measures how well an asset's specific characteristics (its "factors") align with the current market regime. 

## How ARCS Works

The Asset-to-Regime Compatibility Score (ARCS) evaluates the asset across five dimensions and weights them based on what the *current* regime demands:

- In **Risk-On** regimes, the engine heavily rewards Momentum and Trend.
- In **Defensive** regimes, the engine shifts focus and heavily rewards Low Volatility and high Liquidity.
- In **Neutral** or **Transitioning** regimes, the engine balances all factors to seek all-weather resilience.

## Factor Profiles

Every asset exhibits dominant factors:
- **Momentum/Growth**: High beta, fast-moving. Thrives in Risk-On.
- **Quality/Defensive**: Low volatility, highly liquid, stable sentiment. Thrives in Defensive/Risk-Off.
- **Contrarian**: Negative trend but surging sentiment and volume.

## What It Means

- **Strong Fit**: The asset possesses exactly the traits the current market environment rewards. A low-volatility utility stock in a Defensive regime, or a high-momentum crypto token in a Strong Risk-On regime. This provides a massive bonus to the final Signal Strength.
- **Weak Fit / Misaligned**: The asset is fighting the tape. A highly volatile, low-liquidity small-cap stock during a Risk-Off panic. Even if its individual chart looks okay, its Signal Strength is heavily penalized because the systemic risk of failure is too high.
