## Key Concept

**Peer Divergence** occurs when an asset's behavior breaks away from its sector or asset class peers. If the banking sector is rallying but one bank's scores are deteriorating, that divergence is a signal worth investigating. The Discovery feed surfaces these moments because they often reveal information that broad market moves obscure.

Divergence is measured by comparing an asset's score changes against the median score changes of its peer group over the same period. A divergence of 20+ points across multiple scores is considered significant.

## What It Means

- **Positive divergence (asset outperforming peers)**: The asset is strengthening while its sector weakens, or strengthening *more* than its sector. This could signal emerging leadership, a company-specific catalyst, or early recognition of improving fundamentals.
- **Negative divergence (asset underperforming peers)**: The asset is weakening while its sector holds steady or strengthens. This could signal company-specific problems, deteriorating fundamentals, or early warning of issues the market hasn't fully priced.
- **Temporary divergence**: Short-lived divergences (1-3 days) are often noise — earnings dates, index rebalancing, or one-off news events. These tend to mean-revert.
- **Persistent divergence**: When an asset diverges from peers for 2+ weeks across multiple scores, something structural has likely changed. These are the most analytically valuable signals.

The most powerful insight from peer divergence: it separates *company-specific* signals from *sector-wide* signals. If every tech stock is falling, a tech stock falling isn't news. But if every tech stock is falling *except one*, that exception deserves attention.

## What It Does NOT Mean

- Positive divergence does **not** mean the asset is a buy. The asset might be diverging because of a temporary anomaly, not a sustainable advantage.
- Negative divergence does **not** mean the asset is doomed. It might be a temporary dislocation that creates a buying opportunity — or it might be an early warning of real trouble. Investigation is required.
- Peer divergence is **not** a standalone signal. It tells you *something is different* about this asset relative to its group. Understanding *why* requires looking at the other scores, news, and fundamentals.
