## Key Concept

The **Discovery Feed** surfaces assets that are experiencing meaningful change — not just price movement. Each card is powered by a **Discovery Relevance Score (DRS)**, a 0–100 composite that identifies when something genuinely interesting is happening to an asset.

Unlike a stock screener that filters by static criteria, the DRS detects *change* — score inflections, peer divergence, regime sensitivity shifts, and structural anomalies. It answers: "What deserves my attention right now, and why?"

## What It Means

Each discovery card contains several key elements:

- **DRS Score** — The overall relevance score. Higher means more significant change detected. Scores below 25 are suppressed as noise.
- **Archetype Badge** — Tells you *why* this asset surfaced:
  - `Score Inflection` — A key score (trend, momentum, trust, etc.) shifted significantly in recent days.
  - `Peer Divergence` — This asset is behaving differently from its sector or type peers.
  - `Regime Sensitive` — The asset's characteristics make it particularly relevant given the current market regime.
  - `Sentiment Shift` — Unusual evidence or news activity detected.
  - `Structural Anomaly` — Something changed in the asset's structure (ETF concentration shift, MF style drift, crypto on-chain divergence).
- **Headline** — A one-line summary of what changed, generated from templates (not AI).
- **Context** — Two lines explaining why this matters in the current environment.
- **Score Pills** — Key engine scores (Trend, Momentum, Volatility, etc.) so you can quickly assess the asset's current state.

## What It Does NOT Mean

- A high DRS does **not** mean "buy this asset." It means something interesting changed that's worth understanding.
- Discovery cards are **not** recommendations. They surface analytical signals, not trading signals.
- The feed is **not** a ranking of "best" assets. An asset with DRS 90 isn't "better" than one with DRS 60 — it's experiencing more significant change right now.
- Suppressed items (DRS < 25) are **not** necessarily bad. They simply aren't showing meaningful change at this moment. Stable, well-performing assets often have low DRS because nothing is changing.

## Quick Check

- [x] The DRS measures meaningful change, not whether an asset is "good" or "bad." | Correct — DRS detects inflections, divergence, and anomalies. A falling stock with a score inflection will surface just like a rising one.
- [ ] A high DRS score means you should buy the asset. | No — DRS surfaces what's changing, not what to trade. Always do your own analysis.
- [x] The archetype badge tells you why an asset appeared in the discovery feed. | Yes — it categorizes the type of change detected (score inflection, peer divergence, regime sensitivity, etc.).
- [ ] If an asset doesn't appear in the feed, it means nothing is happening with it. | Not necessarily — it might be stable and performing well. Low DRS means low *change*, not low quality.
