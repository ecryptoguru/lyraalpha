## Key Concept

Commodities are fundamentally **macro-driven assets**. Unlike stocks, which respond to company earnings, or crypto, which responds to network activity, commodity prices are shaped by inflation expectations, currency movements, interest rates, and geopolitical events.

**Regime sensitivity** measures how a commodity's returns change across different market environments. Gold behaves very differently in a Risk-Off regime than in a Risk-On regime. Crude oil responds to growth expectations. Agricultural commodities follow their own seasonal and supply-driven cycles.

Our Commodity Intelligence Engine computes a **regime sensitivity profile** for each commodity by analyzing its historical returns during each market regime state.

## What It Means

Each commodity gets a profile showing average daily returns across four regime states:

- **Risk-On** — Growth-sensitive commodities (oil, copper, industrial metals) tend to perform well. Precious metals may lag.
- **Neutral** — Most commodities trade on their own supply/demand fundamentals rather than macro sentiment.
- **Defensive** — Gold and silver often begin to outperform as investors seek hedges. Energy may weaken on growth concerns.
- **Risk-Off** — Gold is the classic safe haven. Oil typically drops on demand destruction fears. Agricultural commodities are less correlated.

The engine also computes:
- **Inflation Sensitivity** — How strongly the commodity correlates with inflation expectations. Gold and oil are historically inflation-sensitive; agricultural commodities less so.
- **USD Sensitivity** — Most commodities are priced in dollars, so a stronger USD typically pressures commodity prices. Gold has the strongest inverse USD relationship.
- **Safe Haven Score** — How well the commodity performs specifically during Risk-Off periods relative to the broader universe.

## What It Does NOT Mean

- Regime sensitivity profiles are based on **historical patterns**. Past behavior during Risk-Off periods does not guarantee the same behavior in future Risk-Off periods.
- A high safe haven score does **not** mean the commodity will protect your portfolio in every downturn. Gold failed as a safe haven during the March 2020 liquidity crisis, for example.
- Commodity regime analysis is **not** a price prediction. Knowing that oil tends to drop during Risk-Off doesn't tell you when or by how much.
- These profiles use **daily return averages**, which smooth out significant intra-day and intra-week volatility. Actual experience during regime transitions can be much rougher.

- [ ] Gold always goes up during Risk-Off periods. | No — while gold historically performs well in Risk-Off, there are exceptions (e.g., liquidity crises where everything sells). Historical patterns are not guarantees.
- [x] USD sensitivity matters for commodities because most are priced in dollars. | Yes — a stronger dollar typically pressures commodity prices, and this relationship varies by commodity.
- [ ] If oil has high regime sensitivity to Risk-On, it will definitely rise when the market enters Risk-On. | No — regime sensitivity describes historical tendencies, not certainties. Supply disruptions, OPEC decisions, and other factors can override regime effects.
