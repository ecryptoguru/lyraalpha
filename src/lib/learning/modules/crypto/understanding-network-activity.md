## Key Concept

**Network Activity** measures the real usage of a blockchain — active addresses, transaction counts, transfer volumes, and smart contract interactions. It's the on-chain equivalent of foot traffic in a store: high activity suggests genuine demand, while declining activity may signal waning interest regardless of price.

Unlike price, which can be manipulated by a few large trades, network activity reflects the behavior of thousands of participants. It's one of the most honest signals in crypto.

## What It Means

- **Rising activity + rising price**: Healthy growth. Real users are driving demand. This is the strongest bullish configuration.
- **Rising activity + flat/falling price**: Accumulation phase. Users are active but price hasn't responded yet. Often precedes breakouts.
- **Falling activity + rising price**: Speculative rally. Price is being driven by trading, not usage. This divergence is a warning sign.
- **Falling activity + falling price**: Genuine decline. Both usage and price are contracting. The network is losing relevance.

Developer activity (GitHub commits, protocol upgrades) is a leading indicator — it shows whether builders are still investing in the ecosystem.

## What It Does NOT Mean

- High network activity does **not** guarantee price appreciation. Activity can be high due to panic selling, airdrop farming, or bot activity.
- Low activity does **not** mean the project is dead. Some protocols have low transaction counts by design (e.g., Bitcoin's store-of-value thesis doesn't require high daily transactions).
- Network activity metrics are **not** immune to manipulation. Wash trading and bot-generated transactions can inflate activity numbers artificially.
