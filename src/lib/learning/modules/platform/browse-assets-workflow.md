## Key Concept

The asset browser is not just a ticker list. It is the **entry point into deeper analysis**. The workflow is to screen broadly, shortlist quickly, then open the asset page only for names that deserve more attention.

Think of it as a funnel: browse first, investigate second.

## What It Means

- Use the browser to scan for **candidates**, not to make final decisions.
- Open the asset page when something stands out: a strong setup, unusual divergence, or a regime-fit question.
- On the asset page, move in sequence: **quick explanation → technical/context blocks → deeper analysis**.
- If two names look similar, move into Compare or Lyra instead of guessing from the browser grid alone.

## What It Does NOT Mean

- The browser is **not** a substitute for the full asset page.
- A strong-looking tile is **not** enough on its own; it is a prompt to investigate.
- More scanning is **not** always better. Once a name is interesting, switch into deeper analysis.

- [ ] Opening the full asset page is only necessary for risky assets like crypto. | No — every asset type benefits from deeper context once it reaches your shortlist.
