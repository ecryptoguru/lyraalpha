## Key Concept

Lyra is most useful when you treat it like an **analysis partner**, not a search bar. The goal is not to ask one giant question and hope for a perfect answer. The goal is to move from a broad question to a sharper follow-up using the evidence already on the page.

A good Lyra workflow usually starts with a clear asset, portfolio, or market context, then narrows into one decision: conviction, risk, regime fit, or comparison.

## What It Means

- Start with a **specific object**: an asset, a portfolio, or a current market theme.
- Ask for **explanation before action**: "Why is this score high?" is usually better than "Should I buy this?"
- Use follow-ups to reduce ambiguity: ask what changed, what is strongest, what is weakest, and what risk matters most.
- Use Lyra after reading the page once. The best questions come from visible tension in the data.

## What It Does NOT Mean

- Lyra is **not** a prediction engine and does not guarantee outcomes.
- A confident answer is **not** personal financial advice.
- The best workflow is **not** asking longer prompts. It is asking clearer prompts.
