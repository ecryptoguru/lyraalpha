## Key Concept

**Regime Transition Patterns** describe the recurring sequences through which markets move from one regime to another. While the *timing* of transitions is unpredictable, the *sequence* of events during transitions follows recognizable patterns. Understanding these patterns helps you identify transitions earlier and position accordingly.

Markets don't flip like a switch from risk-on to crisis. They transition through stages, and each stage has characteristic signals across asset classes, volatility, credit markets, and cross-asset correlations.

## What It Means

- **Risk-On → Transitional**: Early warning signs include credit spread widening while equities remain elevated, declining market breadth (fewer stocks participating in the rally), rising put/call ratios, and small-cap underperformance. The VIX may still be low, but the "internals" are deteriorating.
- **Transitional → Risk-Off**: Confirmation signals include equity indices breaking key support levels, bond yields falling (flight to quality), gold strengthening, and volatility expanding. Sector rotation from cyclicals to defensives accelerates.
- **Risk-Off → Crisis**: Escalation signals include credit market freezing (spreads blowing out), correlation convergence (everything selling together), liquidity evaporating in normally liquid markets, and central bank emergency interventions.
- **Crisis → Recovery**: Recovery signals include central bank policy response, credit spreads stabilizing then tightening, high-yield bonds outperforming treasuries, and small-cap/high-beta stocks beginning to outperform. The VIX peaks and begins declining.
- **Recovery → Risk-On**: Confirmation includes broadening market participation, declining volatility, tightening credit spreads, and cyclical sector leadership. This is often the strongest return period — but also when complacency begins building for the next cycle.

The critical insight: transitions are *processes*, not events. They unfold over weeks to months, giving observant investors time to adjust — if they know what to look for.

## What It Does NOT Mean

- Recognizing a transition pattern does **not** mean you can time the exact turning point. Transitions can stall, reverse, or accelerate unpredictably.
- Historical transition patterns are **not** guaranteed to repeat identically. Each cycle has unique characteristics driven by the specific catalyst.
- Transition awareness is **not** a substitute for risk management. Even if you correctly identify a transition, the magnitude and speed of the move can exceed expectations.
