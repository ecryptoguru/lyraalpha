# Crypto Sector Analytical Playbooks

This document provides sector-specific analytical frameworks for the major crypto sectors represented in the LyraAlpha AI universe. Use these playbooks to deliver sector-aware analysis — a Layer 1 blockchain and a DeFi protocol require fundamentally different analytical lenses even when their engine scores are identical.

---

## Layer 1 Blockchains (BTC, ETH, SOL, AVAX, ADA, DOT)

### Key Metrics (beyond engine scores)
- **Network Activity**: Active addresses, transaction count, network utilization. Rising activity + rising price = confirmed demand. Rising price + falling activity = speculative rally (fragile).
- **On-Chain Metrics**: Exchange net flow (negative = HODLing bullish), SOPR (above 1 = profit-taking, below 1 = capitulation), MVRV ratio (>3.5 = overheated, <1 = undervalued).
- **Staking Economics**: For PoS chains (ETH, SOL, AVAX) — staking yield, % of supply staked. High staking = strong conviction but reduces circulating supply.
- **Developer Ecosystem**: GitHub commits, active developers, protocol upgrades. >50 active developers = healthy ecosystem.
- **Security Metrics**: Network hashrate (PoW), validator count (PoS), decentralization score. More validators = more decentralized but potentially slower.

### Consensus & Performance Trade-offs
- **Proof of Work (BTC)**: Most battle-tested, energy-intensive, 10-min block times, 60-min finality. Store-of-value narrative.
- **Proof of Stake (ETH, SOL, AVAX)**: 99.9% more energy efficient, faster finality (seconds), staking rewards. ETH has dual narrative (tech platform + monetary asset).
- **Blockchain Trilemma**: Cannot simultaneously maximize security, decentralization, AND scalability. Each chain makes explicit trade-offs.

### Valuation Context
- **NVT Ratio** (Network Value / Transaction Volume): High NVT = overvalued vs usage, low NVT = undervalued.
- **Stock-to-Flow** (BTC only): Models scarcity based on halving schedule. Historically predictive but increasingly debated at scale.
- **Realized Cap**: Sum of all tokens valued at last-moved price. MVRV = Market Cap / Realized Cap.
- **FDV/MCap Ratio**: >5x = heavy future dilution (structural headwind). <0.8 = most tokens already circulating.

## DeFi Protocols (UNI, AAVE, MKR, CRV, LDO, SUSHI)

### Key Metrics
- **TVL (Total Value Locked)**: Peak ~$180B (Nov 2021), trough ~$37B (Dec 2022), recovery ~$90-100B. TVL/MCap ratio measures valuation vs usage.
- **Protocol Revenue**: Fees generated by the protocol. Check if fees are passed to token holders (value accrual) or burned.
- **Revenue/TVL**: Capital efficiency metric. Higher = more fees per dollar locked.
- **Impermanent Loss**: AMM LP risk when pooled asset prices diverge. IL is offset by trading fee income.
- **Smart Contract Risk**: Audits reduce but don't eliminate risk. Major hacks can wipe 50-90% of TVL.

### DeFi Sub-Sectors
- **DEX (Uniswap, Curve)**: Revenue from trading fees. Volume and fee tier structure matter.
- **Lending (AAVE, Compound)**: Revenue from interest spread. Liquidation cascades are tail risk.
- **Liquid Staking (Lido, Rocket Pool)**: Revenue from staking yield split. Centralization concerns for Lido (32% ETH staked).
- **Yield Aggregators (Yearn)**: Complex strategy vaults. Strategy risk + underlying protocol risk.

### TVL/MCap Interpretation
- **TVL/MCap > 1.0**: Undervalued relative to economic activity. Rare and potentially bullish.
- **TVL/MCap 0.3-1.0**: Fair value range for DeFi protocols.
- **TVL/MCap < 0.1**: Speculative premium. Market cap driven by narrative, not usage.
- **TVL = 0 or N/A**: Not a DeFi protocol (e.g., BTC, payment tokens). Don't penalize for this.

## Layer 2 Scaling Solutions (ARB, OP, IMX, BASE, MATIC)

### Key Metrics
- **Transaction Volume**: Daily tx count and unique addresses. Growth = adoption.
- **Gas Cost Savings**: 10-100x cheaper than Ethereum mainnet. Lower fees = more economic activity.
- **Sequencer Revenue**: L2s typically earn from sequencer fees. Centralized sequencer = centralization risk.
- **Bridge TVL**: Assets bridged from L1. Higher bridge TVL = more ecosystem activity.
- **Token Utility**: Often limited to governance. Check if there's a value accrual mechanism.

### L1 Dependency Risk
- All L2s depend on their parent L1 (usually Ethereum). L1 security issues directly affect L2 viability.
- L2s inherit L1's monetary policy (ETH deflationary via EIP-1559 burn).
- L2 success = L1 success (positive correlation, not zero-sum).

### Valuation Nuances
- L2 tokens often have limited utility (governance only). Pure governance tokens without fee accrual have structurally weak value capture.
- Compare L2 TVL growth vs L1 TVL growth. Outperformance = L2 gaining market share.

## Stablecoins & Monetary Dynamics (USDT, USDC, DAI, USDS)

### Key Metrics
- **Market Cap**: USDT ~$110B, USDC ~$35B, DAI/USDS ~$5B. Market cap = capital on sidelines.
- **Backing**: Fiat-backed (USDT, USDC) vs crypto-collateralized (DAI) vs algorithmic (failed).
- **Stablecoin Dominance**: Rising = capital on sidelines = cautious near-term but bullish medium-term (dry powder).
- **Stablecoin Supply Ratio**: BTC MCap / stablecoin supply. Low ratio = high buying power = bullish.

### Types & Risks
- **Fiat-backed (USDT, USDC)**: 1:1 backed by USD/treasuries. Issuer counterparty risk. Tether reserves transparency concerns.
- **Crypto-collateralized (DAI)**: Over-collateralized with ETH/BTC. Decentralized but capital-inefficient.
- **Algorithmic (UST/LUNA)**: Catastrophically failed May 2022 — $40B wiped in 72 hours. Avoid.

### India Context
- Stablecoins subject to same 30% tax on trades as other crypto.
- India has strict crypto tax regime — has driven trading volume offshore.

## Gaming & Metaverse (AXS, SAND, MANA, IMX)

### Key Metrics
- **User Metrics**: DAU (Daily Active Users), MAU (Monthly Active Users). These are consumer products — user growth matters most.
- **Token Economics**: Often inflationary by design (play-to-earn rewards). Check emission schedules.
- **NFT Floor Prices**: Secondary market activity indicator for game assets.
- **Partnerships**: Brand partnerships (IP collaborations) drive user acquisition.

### Narrative Sensitivity
- Highly narrative-driven. Sentiment score extremes are more meaningful here than in DeFi.
- Gaming cycles tied to broader crypto cycles but can decouple during metaverse hype waves.
- Quality filters: FDV/MCap >5x, age <6 months, no security audits = avoid.

### Valuation Nuances
- Traditional crypto metrics (TVL, on-chain activity) less relevant than user metrics.
- Revenue from NFT marketplace fees, in-game purchases, token sales.
- High structural risk due to category immaturity.

## AI & Compute (FET, RENDER, TAO, RNDR)

### Key Metrics
- **Adoption Metrics**: Compute hours, API calls, network utilization when available.
- **Node Economics**: For decentralized compute networks — node rewards, utilization rates.
- **Model Training**: Number of models trained, dataset size.
- **Partnerships**: Integration with AI companies, model providers.

### Emerging Category Risks
- High structural risk due to category immaturity. Maturity risk component of Structural Risk especially relevant.
- Correlation with AI narrative in equities (NVDA, AMD) — when AI hype fades in stocks, these tokens often sell off harder.
- Many projects are pre-revenue — valued on future potential, not current metrics.

### Valuation Framework
- Protocol revenue vs token holder revenue — check if value accrues to token.
- Network effects: more compute providers = lower costs = more demand = flywheel.
- Competition from centralized AI (AWS, Google Cloud) is structural headwind.

## Meme & Community Tokens (DOGE, SHIB, PEPE)

### Key Metrics
- **Community Size**: Social media followers, engagement, sentiment.
- **Liquidity**: 24h volume >1% of market cap required for meaningful position.
- **Holder Distribution**: Top 10 wallets >40% = manipulation risk.
- **Burn Mechanisms**: Token burns reduce supply — check if implemented and sustainable.

### Quality Filters
- **FDV/MCap >5x**: Heavy future dilution — structural headwind.
- **Age <6 months**: Newer, untested.
- **No security audits**: Higher smart contract risk.
- **Anonymous teams**: Higher rug pull risk.
- **Emission rate >20% annual**: Structural sell pressure.

### India Context
- Only ~10-15 altcoins have sufficient INR liquidity on CoinDCX/WazirX for sizeable positions without material slippage.
- Meme tokens particularly risky due to regulatory uncertainty.

## Interpreting Engine Scores by Crypto Sector

The same engine score means different things in different crypto sectors:

| Score Pattern | Layer 1 | DeFi | L2 | Stablecoins |
|:-------------|:---------|:-----|:---|:------------|
| T:80+ | Strong network adoption | TVL growth inflow | Transaction volume surge | Capital rotation into crypto |
| V:70+ | Normal for crypto (volatile) | Concerning for lending protocols | Normal during network congestion | Should be low-vol by design |
| L:40- | Concerning for any crypto | Very concerning for DeFi (liquidity risk) | Common for newer L2s | Red flag for stablecoins |
| M:30- | Network activity declining | TVL outflow | User adoption slowing | Stablecoin supply shrinking |

### Sector-Regime Interaction
- **RISK_ON**: Altcoins outperform, DeFi TVL expands, gaming tokens rally.
- **DEFENSIVE**: BTC and ETH outperform alts, stablecoin dominance rises.
- **RISK_OFF**: Flight to BTC and stablecoins, DeFi TVL contracts, high-beta alts sell off hardest.
- **Sector dispersion**: High dispersion = sector selection matters. Low dispersion = macro dominates everything.
